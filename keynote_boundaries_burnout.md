## 📜 FULL KEYNOTE SCRIPT

### OPENING HOOK (5 minutes)

*[Walk to center stage, pause, make eye contact]*

"Raise your hand if you've ever been on a rescue mission where someone didn't follow protocol... and it nearly cost them everything."

*[Pause for hands]*

"Now keep your hand up if YOU have ever been that person... not on a mission, but in your own life. Running on empty. Ignoring the warning signs. Telling yourself 'I'm fine' when everything inside is screaming 'MAYDAY.'"

*[Pause, smile knowingly]*

"Yeah. Me too. Welcome to the club none of us wanted to join."

*[Warm smile]*

"My name is Chelsea Flynn. I'm a Catholic speaker, a life transformation coach, a mother of three—including a child with special needs—and a woman who once thought burnout was a badge of honor. Spoiler alert: it's not. It's a burial plot we dig for ourselves while pretending we're building a garden."

"Today, we're going to talk about something you know intimately as Search and Rescue professionals: the art of saving lives. But here's the twist—we're going to talk about saving YOUR life. Because here's what I've learned..."

*[Lean in, lower voice]*

"You cannot rescue anyone if you are drowning yourself."

---

### PERSONAL STORY: MY BURNOUT MOMENT (8 minutes)

"Let me take you back to my chaos moment. Picture this: I'm a mother of three. My youngest has autism—beautiful, brilliant, and requiring constant attention and care. I'm running a business. I'm trying to be everything to everyone. I'm saying yes to every request because I thought that's what strong women do."

"I was the person everyone called when they needed something. The fixer. The helper. The one who shows up. Sound familiar to anyone in this room?"

*[Knowing looks with audience]*

"One morning, I woke up and couldn't get out of bed. Not 'didn't want to'—COULDN'T. My body had finally staged a full rebellion against the dictatorship I'd been running. My mind was foggy. My soul felt hollow. I looked at my phone—47 unread messages—and I felt nothing but dread."

"I had become so good at rescuing everyone else that I had completely abandoned myself."

*[Pause]*

"Here's what nobody tells you about burnout: it doesn't announce itself. It doesn't send a calendar invite. It creeps in like fog until suddenly you can't see your own hand in front of your face."

"For you—women who literally save lives for a living—this is even more dangerous. Because your job requires you to be 'on' at a moment's notice. You can't say 'Sorry, I'm burned out' when someone is trapped in a car or lost in the wilderness."

"But here's the truth bomb I had to learn the hard way: **Boundaries aren't selfish. They're survival equipment.**"

---

### INTERACTIVE ACTIVITY #1: THE OXYGEN MASK CHECK (7 minutes)

"Alright, let's get real. I want everyone to stand up."

*[Wait for everyone to stand]*

"We're going to do a quick Oxygen Mask Check. I'm going to read some statements. If the statement is TRUE for you RIGHT NOW, I want you to take one step backward. If it's false, stay where you are. Be honest—this room is a judgment-free zone."

**Read these statements (pause between each):**

1. "I have trouble falling asleep or staying asleep because my mind won't stop."
2. "I can't remember the last time I did something purely for fun—not for anyone else, just for me."
3. "I feel guilty when I take time for myself."
4. "I've snapped at someone I love recently because I was overwhelmed."
5. "I've said 'I'm fine' in the past week when I wasn't."
6. "I feel like I'm running on fumes but keep going anyway."
7. "I've neglected a doctor's appointment, meal, or basic self-care because I was 'too busy.'"

*[Look around the room]*

"Look around you. Look how far back some of us have moved. This isn't shame—this is awareness. This is your dashboard warning light. And unlike in Search and Rescue, we're allowed to acknowledge when OUR tank is low."

*[Have everyone return to seats]*

"Here's the good news: awareness is the first step to change. You can't rescue yourself from a building you don't know you're trapped in."

---

### TEACHING: THE 3 B'S OF BURNOUT (10 minutes)

"In my work as a coach, I've identified what I call the **3 B's of Burnout**. These are the three areas where we hemorrhage our energy without even realizing it."

**Write on flip chart or display slide:**

## THE 3 B'S OF BURNOUT

### 1. BLURRED BOUNDARIES
"This is when we can't tell where we end and everyone else begins. We take on other people's emergencies as our own. We answer work calls at dinner. We say yes when every fiber of our being screams no."

"For Search and Rescue professionals, this is especially tricky because your job literally requires you to respond to emergencies. But here's the distinction: **There's a difference between being responsive and being responsible for everything.**"

### 2. BURIED NEEDS
"This is when we push our own needs so far down that we forget they exist. When's the last time you asked yourself: What do I need right now? Not what does my team need. Not what does my family need. What do YOU need?"

"We bury our needs because we've been taught that good women are selfless. But selfless isn't a virtue when it leads to self-destruction."

### 3. BROKEN BOUNDARIES
"These are the boundaries we set and then immediately violate. We say 'I'm not checking email after 6pm' and then check it at 6:01. We say 'I need a day off' and then fill it with errands for everyone else."

*[Pause]*

"Which of these B's resonates most with you right now? Hold that thought—we're going to work on it."

---

### INTERACTIVE ACTIVITY #2: BOUNDARY AUDIT (10 minutes)

"Find a partner. Someone you don't know well is even better. You have 5 minutes each—so 10 minutes total."

*[Wait for pairs to form]*

"Here's your task. Take turns answering these three questions with your partner:"

**Display or read:**

1. **"What is one boundary I KNOW I need to set but haven't?"**
   (Examples: Not checking email after hours, saying no to extra shifts, asking for help)

2. **"What am I afraid will happen if I set this boundary?"**
   (Be honest—what's the fear? Rejection? Being seen as weak? Letting people down?)

3. **"What is ALREADY happening because I HAVEN'T set this boundary?"**
   (Exhaustion? Resentment? Health issues? Strained relationships?)

"Person A goes first—5 minutes. Then switch. Your partner's job is just to listen. No fixing. No advice. Just witness."

*[Set timer for 5 minutes, announce switch, set timer again]*

*[After activity]*

"How did that feel? Sometimes just saying it out loud to another human makes it real. And when it's real, we can change it."

---

### TEACHING: BUILDING YOUR RESCUE PLAN (8 minutes)

"In Search and Rescue, you never go into a situation without a plan. You assess the terrain. You identify the risks. You know your exit strategy."

"Why don't we do this for our own lives?"

"I want to introduce you to what I call the **Personal Rescue Plan**—a framework for setting boundaries that actually stick."

**Display or write:**

## THE R.E.S.C.U.E. FRAMEWORK

**R - RECOGNIZE** the warning signs
- What does YOUR burnout look like? Irritability? Insomnia? Getting sick constantly? Know your red flags.

**E - ESTABLISH** your non-negotiables
- What are 2-3 things you MUST protect? (We'll do this next)

**S - SPEAK** your boundaries out loud
- A boundary that lives only in your head isn't a boundary. It's a wish.

**C - CREATE** accountability
- Tell someone. Write it down. Make it visible.

**U - UNDERSTAND** that boundaries require maintenance
- You don't set a boundary once and forget it. You reinforce it. Daily.

**E - EXPECT** pushback
- People who benefited from your lack of boundaries will not celebrate your new ones. That's okay. Your health is not up for negotiation.

---

### INTERACTIVE ACTIVITY #3: THE NON-NEGOTIABLES (7 minutes)

*[Hand out index cards or have them use their phones]*

"On your card or phone, I want you to write down THREE non-negotiables. These are the things you are committing to protect, starting TODAY."

"Make them specific and actionable. Not 'take better care of myself' but 'I will not answer work calls after 8pm' or 'I will take my full lunch break' or 'I will ask for help when I'm overwhelmed.'"

*[Give 3 minutes to write]*

"Now, here's the brave part. I want three volunteers to share ONE of their non-negotiables with the room."

*[Take 3 volunteers]*

"When we speak our boundaries out loud, they become real. These women just made a commitment in front of 39 witnesses. That takes courage."

*[To volunteers]*

"You've just done something powerful. You've moved from intention to declaration."

---

### CLOSING & CALL TO ACTION (5 minutes)

"Let me leave you with this."

"In my journey from chaos to Christ, from burnout to breakthrough, I learned that transformation doesn't happen in one dramatic moment. It happens in the small daily choices to honor ourselves."

*[Slow down, heartfelt]*

"You are women who run toward danger when everyone else runs away. You are the ones who show up at 3am. You are the calm in someone else's worst moment. That is extraordinary."

"But you are also human beings who need rest. Who need joy. Who need boundaries. And setting those boundaries doesn't make you less of a rescuer—it makes you a sustainable one."

"Here's my challenge to you: **This week, enforce ONE boundary. Just one.** See what happens. Notice how it feels. And when that voice in your head says 'Who do you think you are?'—you answer: 'I am someone worth rescuing too.'"

*[Final pause]*

"Remember: He turns our mess into a message, our tests into testimonies. Your burnout doesn't have to be the end of your story. It can be the beginning of a new chapter—one where you show up for yourself as fiercely as you show up for everyone else."

"Thank you for the work you do. Thank you for being here. And thank you for giving yourself this hour to invest in YOU."

*[Pause]*

"Now go rescue yourselves. You've earned it."

## 💡 SPEAKER NOTES

**Energy Management:**
- Start high energy, vary throughout
- Lower voice for emotional moments
- Use movement—don't stay behind podium
- Make eye contact with different sections of room

**Audience Connection:**
- These women have seen trauma—be respectful of that
- Acknowledge their sacrifice early and often
- Use "we" language—you're in this with them
- Humor helps, but keep it relatable (not performative)

**Flexibility:**
- If an activity runs long, shorten speaking sections
- If energy is low, add more movement
- Read the room—adjust depth of personal sharing accordingly

**Follow-Up Opportunity:**
- Mention your coaching services briefly at end
- Have business cards or QR code available
- Offer a free discovery call for anyone who wants to go deeper
